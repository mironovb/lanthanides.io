<div class="element-section info-block">
  <h2 class="section-title">Important Notes</h2>
  <div class="notes-block">
    <div class="note-item note-warning">
      <strong>Export controls in force</strong>
      Samarium is one of the seven medium and heavy rare earths controlled under MOFCOM/GAC Announcement No.&thinsp;18/2025. SmCo magnet alloys and finished magnet rotors are explicitly named in the controlled item list [2].
    </div>
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>SmCo &gt; NdFeB at high temperature</strong>
      Samarium-cobalt magnets retain useful coercivity to 350&thinsp;&deg;C, well above NdFeB&rsquo;s ~200&thinsp;&deg;C ceiling. This makes SmCo irreplaceable in missile guidance, satellite actuators, and high-temperature servo motors. There is no engineering substitute.
    </div>
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>Co-production constraint</strong>
      Samarium is recovered as a by-product of Nd/Pr separation from bastnaesite. Supply cannot scale independently of light rare earth output, meaning Sm prices respond more to magnet-grade Nd demand than to Sm-specific demand.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
