  <div class="banner-title">Suspended Export Control</div>
  <div class="banner-body">
    <p>Europium was added to China&rsquo;s expanded heavy rare earth control scope under the October&thinsp;2025 escalation announcements [2]. The Eu licensing requirement was suspended on 7&ndash;9&thinsp;November&thinsp;2025 by MOFCOM Announcements Nos.&thinsp;70 and 72 of 2025 [3].</p>
    <p>Suspension runs until 28&thinsp;November&thinsp;2026. The underlying No.&thinsp;18/2025 framework still touches Eu indirectly via mixed-rare-earth metal and alloy classifications, so customs treatment can be inconsistent even during the suspension window.</p>

<div class="element-section info-block">
  <h2 class="section-title">Verified Offers</h2>
  <p class="section-intro">Europium oxide trades at unusually low prices for a heavy rare earth (~$30/kg) because the historical fluorescent-lamp demand collapsed faster than the supply chain could re-tool. The European Union banned mercury-containing fluorescent lamps in stages through 2023&ndash;2027, removing the largest single Eu phosphor market.</p>

<div class="element-section info-block">
  <h2 class="section-title">Important Notes</h2>
  <div class="notes-block">
    <div class="note-item note-warning">
      <strong>Snap-back risk on 28&thinsp;November&thinsp;2026</strong>
      The October 2025 controls covering Eu were suspended in November 2025 and expire if not renewed by 28&thinsp;November&thinsp;2026. Pricing currently does not reflect this latent licensing risk.
    </div>
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>Demand collapse without phase-out replacement</strong>
      Eu&rsquo;s historical anchor &mdash; CRT and fluorescent lamp phosphors &mdash; is in terminal decline. White LEDs use far less Eu per unit, and OLED displays use no Eu at all. There is no offsetting growth segment.
    </div>
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>Co-production constraint</strong>
      Eu is recovered as a by-product of light rare earth separation. Producers cannot reduce Eu output without curtailing Nd/Pr supply, leading to chronic Eu oxide overhang.
    </div>
  </div>
