<div class="element-section info-block">
  <h2 class="section-title">Important Notes</h2>
  <div class="notes-block">
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>Co-production with copper</strong>
      Selenium is recovered from copper anode slimes during electrorefining. Production scales with global Cu refining throughput (~25&thinsp;Mt/year), not Se demand. New supply requires modifying anode-slime treatment circuits at integrated Cu refineries.
    </div>
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>Demand softness</strong>
      Two of Se&rsquo;s historical end-uses &mdash; xerographic drums and decorative red glass &mdash; are in long-term decline. CIGS solar growth has not offset legacy demand losses, leaving Se in chronic mild oversupply.
    </div>
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>Toxicity in feed formulation</strong>
      Selenium is essential at micrograms/day but toxic above ~400 &micro;g/day. Animal-feed selenium supplementation operates within tight regulatory thresholds (FDA, EFSA), constraining demand growth from this segment.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
