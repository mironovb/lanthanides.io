<div class="regulatory-banner banner-suspended">
  <div class="banner-title">Suspended Export Control &mdash; Regulation exists but is not enforced</div>
  <div class="banner-body">
    <p>Holmium was <strong>not</strong> included in China&rsquo;s April&thinsp;4, 2025 export controls (Announcement No.&thinsp;18/2025) that restrict Sm, Gd, Tb, Dy, Lu, Sc, and Y [3].</p>
    <p>Holmium <strong>was</strong> added on October&thinsp;9, 2025 (Announcement No.&thinsp;57/2025, item 1C909) covering metallic Ho, Ho alloys, Ho targets, Ho permanent magnets, Ho crystal and magnetic refrigeration materials, and all Ho oxides and compounds [4][5].</p>
    <p>Announcement No.&thinsp;57 was <strong>suspended</strong> before taking effect. MOFCOM Announcement No.&thinsp;70 (7&thinsp;November&thinsp;2025) paused all six October&thinsp;9 announcements until 10&thinsp;November&thinsp;2026 [6]. The regulation is legally intact but unenforced.</p>
    <p>Separately, China&rsquo;s Rare Earth Management Regulations (effective 1&thinsp;October&thinsp;2024) impose domestic traceability, production quotas, and supply-chain controls on all 17 rare earth elements including holmium [7]. These were never suspended.</p>

<div class="element-section info-block">
  <h2 class="section-title">Important Notes</h2>
  <div class="notes-block">
    <div class="note-item note-caution">
      <strong>November 2026 deadline</strong>
      China&rsquo;s October&thinsp;2025 holmium export controls (No.&thinsp;57/2025) are suspended until 10&thinsp;November&thinsp;2026. The regulation is legally intact and can reactivate without new legislation [6]. If reinstated, it covers metallic Ho, alloys, magnets, oxides, and compounds.
    </div>
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>Magnet substitution driver</strong>
      Holmium emerged as a Dy/Tb substitute in NdFeB permanent magnets after April&thinsp;2025 export controls restricted dysprosium and terbium. This demand shift prompted China to add holmium to its October 2025 control list [31]. Continued substitution could fundamentally change holmium&rsquo;s demand profile.
    </div>
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>Extreme retail premium</strong>
      The retail-to-bulk premium for holmium metal is approximately 29&times;. Western retail metal at $2,420/kg compares to Ho<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub> at $82/kg (NE Asia index). This reflects the near-total absence of non-Chinese holmium metal production and the high cost of small-lot reduction processing.
    </div>
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>Supply concentration</strong>
      China supplies 95&ndash;99% of global refined holmium. No non-Chinese project currently produces separated holmium oxide at commercial scale. Lynas and Iluka may add capacity from 2026, but meaningful volumes are not expected before 2027&ndash;2028.
    </div>
