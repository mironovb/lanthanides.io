  <div class="banner-title">Active Export Control</div>
  <div class="banner-body">
    <p>Gallium has been under MOFCOM export licensing since 1&thinsp;August&thinsp;2023 (Announcement No.&thinsp;23/2023), the first of China&rsquo;s critical-mineral export-control orders [2]. On 3&thinsp;December&thinsp;2024, MOFCOM Announcement No.&thinsp;46/2024 banned all dual-use Ga exports to the United States outright [3]; that ban&rsquo;s Article&thinsp;2 was suspended on 9&thinsp;November&thinsp;2025 by Announcement No.&thinsp;72/2025 until 28&thinsp;November&thinsp;2026 [4].</p>
    <p>The general No.&thinsp;23/2023 licensing requirement remains in force. China refines roughly 98% of global primary low-purity gallium.</p>

<div class="element-section info-block">
  <h2 class="section-title">Important Notes</h2>
  <div class="notes-block">
    <div class="note-item note-warning">
      <strong>US dual-use ban suspended, not lifted</strong>
      Article&thinsp;2 of MOFCOM No.&thinsp;46/2024 (the outright Ga dual-use ban targeting US end-users) is suspended until 28&thinsp;November&thinsp;2026 [4]. The general Ga licensing regime under No.&thinsp;23/2023 [2] remains fully active.
    </div>
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>Defence GaN exposure</strong>
      GaN-on-SiC AESA radar transmit/receive modules dominate next-generation airborne and ground radar (F-35, Aegis SPY-6). Strategic gallium supply concerns drove the US National Defense Authorization Act language directing the Defense Logistics Agency to expand Ga stockpiling.
    </div>
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>Co-product constraint</strong>
      Ga is recovered from Bayer-process aluminate liquor at integrated alumina refineries. Production scales with global alumina output (~140&thinsp;Mt/year), not Ga demand. Restarting non-Chinese Ga recovery requires modifying alumina circuits at host refineries.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
