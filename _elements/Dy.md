<div class="element-section info-block">
  <h2 class="section-title">Important Notes</h2>
  <div class="notes-block">
    <div class="note-item note-warning">
      <strong>Export controls in force</strong>
      Dy is one of the seven medium and heavy rare earths controlled under MOFCOM/GAC No.&thinsp;18/2025 [2]. Critically, the order extends to finished NdFeB magnet rotors containing Dy at material thresholds &mdash; not just oxide, metal, or alloy forms.
    </div>
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>EV motor dependency</strong>
      Every NdFeB EV traction motor uses 100&ndash;200&thinsp;g of Dy/Tb to maintain coercivity above the operating temperature of the rotor. Western automaker exposure is direct: a Dy supply disruption would halt EV motor production within months.
    </div>
    <div class="note-item">
      <strong>Grain-boundary diffusion saves Dy</strong>
      Modern manufacturing uses GBD (grain-boundary diffusion) to deposit Dy/Tb only at the magnet grain surfaces, cutting required Dy mass by 30&ndash;50% versus uniform alloying. Hitachi Metals (now Proterial), TDK, and Chinese magnet houses all use GBD processes.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
